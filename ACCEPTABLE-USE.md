# Acceptable Use Policy

## Purpose

This tool is designed for individual developer self-analysis. Its purpose is to help you understand your own AI-assisted coding patterns, measure your productivity, and improve how you work with AI tools — for yourself and, with appropriate consent, your team.

## Prohibited Uses

The following uses are strictly prohibited:

**Covert surveillance**
You may not deploy this tool to monitor, observe, or collect data about other developers without their explicit, informed, and ongoing consent. Running this tool against another person's local session logs, or processing their data on their behalf without their knowledge, is prohibited.

**Exfiltrating proprietary code or prompts**
You may not configure this tool to transmit raw code, full prompt transcripts, proprietary algorithms, or any sensitive intellectual property to external servers, third-party services, or systems outside the user's own machine — except as explicitly described in the privacy policy (aggregated, anonymized summaries sent only to a locally-configured LLM provider of the user's choosing).

**Removing copyright notices**
You may not strip, alter, suppress, or misrepresent the copyright notices, license headers, or attribution statements in this codebase or any derivative works.

**Circumventing the license**
You may not use this software in ways that are inconsistent with its license (AGPL-3.0-or-later), including distributing modified versions without making source code available.

## Permitted Uses

The following uses are explicitly encouraged:

- **Personal productivity analysis** — analyzing your own AI coding sessions to identify patterns, measure quality, reduce wasted effort, and improve prompt discipline.
- **Team analytics with consent** — deploying a shared instance for a team where all team members have been informed, understand what is collected, and have affirmatively agreed.
- **Research and benchmarking with anonymization** — using aggregated, anonymized statistics from this tool for research purposes, academic publications, or public benchmarks, provided no individual's data is identifiable.
- **Building on the codebase** — extending, modifying, or integrating this tool into your own projects, subject to the terms of the project's license (AGPL-3.0-or-later), including the requirement to publish source code for network-accessible modifications.

## Enforcement

Violations of this policy may constitute a breach of the software license and, depending on jurisdiction, may raise additional legal concerns around privacy law, employment law, or computer access statutes. Users are responsible for ensuring their use complies with applicable laws and regulations.
